Metric spaces.

Def. Let $X $ be a set. A metric $d $ on $X $ is a real function such that $d:X\times X\rightarrow \mathbb{R} ^{+} $ such that $\forall x,y,z \in X $:

  1. Positiveness: $d(x,y)\geq 0 $.
  2. $d(x,y)=0 $ iff $x=y $.
  3. Symmetric: $d(x,y)=d(y,x) $.
  4. Triangle inequality: $d(x,z)\leq d(x,y)+d(y,z) $

Lemma Let $(X,d) $ be a metric space. Let $Y\subset X $ be a subset. Then $(Y,d|_Y) $ is also a metric space.

Definition An open ball in a metric space $(X,d) $ of radius $r $ centered at $x $ is the set $B(x,r)=\left\lbrace y\in X|d(x,y)

Definition Let $(X,d) $ be a metric space. Let $Y\subset X $ be a subset of $X $. An element $x\in Y $ is an interior point of $Y $ if $\exists $ $r>0 $ such that $B(x,r)\subset Y $.

Definition A subset $Y\subset X $ is open if $\text{int} (Y)=Y $.

Theorem Let $(X,d) $ be a metric space. Then $B(x,r) $ for $x\in X $ is open in $X $.

Theorem Let $Y $ be a subset of the metric space $(X,d) $. Then $\text{int} (Y) $ is an open subset of $X $.

Theorem The union of a family of open subsets of $(X,d) $ is an open subset of $X $.

Theorem A subset $U $ of a metric space $(X,d) $ is open iff $U $ is a union of open balls in $X $.

Theorem The intersection of any finite number of open subsets of a metric space is open.

Theorem Let $Y $ be a subspace of a metric space $X $. Then a subset $U $ of $Y $ is open in $Y $ iff $U=V\cap Y $ for some open subset $V $ of $X $.

Remark A subset of $X $ that is open in a subspace of $X $ needs not be open in $X $.

Definition Let $Y\subset (X,d) $ be a subset of the metric space. A point $x \in X $ is an Adherent point of (to) $Y $ if $\forall \;r>0 $, $B(x,r)\cap Y\neq \emptyset $.

Definition Let $Y $ be a subset in a metric space $(X,d) $. The Closure of $Y $, denoted $\overline{Y} $, is the set of all adherent points of $Y $.

Remark $Y\subset \overline{Y} $. Visually, $\overline{Y}=Y+\text{boundary} $.

Definition A subset $Y $ is said to be closed if it is equal to its closure: $Y=\overline{Y} $.

Theorem A subset $Y $ of a metric space $X $ is closed iff the complement of $Y $ is open.

Theorem Let $(X,d) $ be a metric space. Let $Y\subset X $. Then $\overline{\overline{Y}}=\overline{Y} $. That is, $\overline{Y} $ is a closed subset.

Theorem The intersection of any family of closed subsets is closed. The union of any finite number of closed subsets is closed.

Remark The infinite union of closed subsets is not necessarily closed.
